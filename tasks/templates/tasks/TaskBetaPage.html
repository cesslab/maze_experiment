Task 2 is similar to task 1. In this task you are given a lottery in which there is a probability of p of receiving the high prize and a probability of (1-p) of receiving the low prize. There are no mazes to solve here, however, the probability of receiving the high prize simply depends on chance. If you get the high prize you get $8 and if you ge the low prize you get $4. Call this "Your Lottery". One key feature of this lottery is that you will not be told what p, the probability of receiving the high prize, is. It can be any number between 0% and 100% and hence the probability of receiving the low prize can also be any number between 0% and 100% depending on what p is. In each of the cases below you must choose between "Your Lottery" and a lottery where the probabilities of receiving the high prize is know and decreases systematically from being 100% in row 1 to 0% in row 11.

Your payoff in this task will be determined as follows: we randomly select one of the 11 cases. If in the selected case you chose Your Lottery, you will be given that lottery, the probability associated with it will be determined, and you will either get the $8 high prize or the $4 low prize. If you choose the Lottery option on the right, you will get either the high or the low prize with the probability stated.

As you look down the column of lottery options you will see that the probability associated with winning the $8 prize systematically decreases while that of winning the $4 prize increases. Your task is to find that row in the table such that for all options above that row you prefer to select, say, the maze option to the lottery but after all rows below you prefer the lottery. By clicking that row you will execute your choice.

For example, say that for cases 1, 2, and 3 you prefer the lottery to the maze option, but for cases (rows) 4 to 11 you prefer the maze. If you click the lottery option in case 3, then the computer will fill the table for you and designate a choice of lottery over maze for cases (rows) 1 to 3 and maze over lottery for cases (rows) 4 to 11 . If you click the maze option in row 7, then that will mean that you prefer the maze option to the lottery for cases (rows) 1 to 7 but the lottery over the maze for cases (rows) 8 to 11. If you always want the maze option, then click the maze option in row 11 and all choices above that will designate the maze option over the lottery. If you always prefer the lottery option, click that option in row 11. To change your choice just click on an option in a different row.
